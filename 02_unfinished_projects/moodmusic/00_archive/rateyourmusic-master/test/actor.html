ToriPower Jun 07 2009
+1 Vote downBookmarkVote up
No puedo evitar escribir unas palabras sobre este segundo disco de Annie Clark que sin duda pasará a erigirse como la banda sonora de este fin de semana de mi vida.

Adorablemente inspirado en sus películas favoritas –desde  Disney hasta Godard-, Actor tiene el perverso mérito de crear un universo tan naif como inquietante, lleno de suavidad y de elegantes clímax ante los que es difícil mantener la indiferencia.

Hay quien critica el excesivo uso de arreglos, pero en mi humilde opinión son un acierto, rescatando a las melodías del tedio y creando un todo coherente, con clase y personalidad.

Al escuchar el disco no tienes la aburrida sensación de haberlo hecho antes, ni la certeza de que no podrías diferenciar sus virtudes de toda esa legión de pequeñas singer-sonwriters indistinguibles en su talento.

Annie es una verdadera artista, como queda demostrado en el hecho de que compone, canta y toca varios instrumentos con envidiable virtuosismo, el resultado es que tanto ella como su creación resultan impregnadas de belleza y elegancia.

A mi me convence, no comparto las opiniones que la consideran una promesa sobrevalorada.

Oníco, cinematográfico, preciosista, con el terror de los cuentos de hadas. En definitiva,  merece la pena darle una oportunidad.

englishmajor Sep 17 2010
+2 Vote downBookmarkVote up
Good, solid, mature songwriting about good, solid, mature themes: child-raising, marital strife, the purpose (if any) of dreams and imagination in an inauthentic and alienating world. That said, while the orchestration works thematically, its often childish tone underscoring and subverting the earnestness and sincerity of the lyrics and the austere art rock "backbone" of these songs, it's also the worst thing about the album: all too often the violins and flutes and so on just sort of flutter aimlessly in a poorly constructed wall of sound, creating a rather irritating backdrop of "busy work" behind what are actually ten very impressive compositions. (This approach nearly sinks what would otherwise be the best song, "Laughing With a Mouth of Blood.") St. Vincent would be better served by a more traditional backing band since—though I hate to say it—she lacks the compositional/orchestral talent of, say, a Sufjan Stevens or a Scott Walker. Still, this is an improvement over Marry Me in every other way, and one assumes (hopes?) that her next album will be the masterpiece I think she's capable of.

ILY May 29 2009
+1 Vote downBookmarkVote up
St. Vincent introduced herself with the playful retro-pop "The Strangers", which reminded a bit of Stereolab's cocktails. She also showed formidable diversity with "Save Me From What I Want", borrowing from lounge and orchestral soundtracks, and displaying jazzy and faux electronic beats. The misshapen trip-hop of "Neighbours" was equally formidable, as if a Portishead remix of Solex's bastard synth-pop. Ditto for the carnival swing "Actor Out Of Work", and the musical theme turned thorny jam "Black Rainbow". Even better, the aggressive chamber synth-pop "Marrow" sounded as if Annie was covering Ligeti, while the warbling ballet "The Bed" and the chamber chanteuse of "The Party" were pure elegance. Then the spook art-pop "Just The Same But Brand New" sounded not unlike a less gothic, but just as dramatic version of Bat For Lashes, and the spiraling ending was sheer class. Clearly art-pop was staging a convincing comeback in 2009.

blair23 May 06 2009
+1 Vote downBookmarkVote up
St. Vincent proves yet again how inventive she truly is. Actor is a showcase in the truest sense of the word. It's filled with varied soundscapes. We are treated a parade of musical ideas and ventures which sound horrifying and exquisitely beautiful at the same time. We swoon at every melody. Every vocal turn touch a nerve. Carnivalesque is an easy word to use for this collection. Every song is a lush escapade. There are songs which sound like stormy revolutions. Some sound like hushed aftermaths. Every sound and every instrument is pieced together delicately as if one wrong move will derail the whole puzzle. It's a cerebral affair which manages to scale emotional heights and the lady at the center of it all could not bore us if she tried.
